Welcome to Baltimore!

Introduction

Baltimore, also known as "Charm City," is a vibrant midsized city nestled in the heart of the state of Maryland. 620,000 people from all walks of life call this city home. I spent the first eighteen years of my life in Baltimore and, as a result, I have truly come to appreciate all that it has to offer. On this webpage, I intend to describe three things that I love about Baltimore, Maryland: blue crabs, the Inner Harbor, and the Ravens.

Blue Crabs

BEFORE                                                  AFTER

Blue Crabs, which happen to be my favorite food in the world, are something of a local delicacy in Baltimore. The crabs are caught in the Chesapeake Bay (America's largest estuary), steamed alive in a pot, and covered in Old Bay, a special seasoning that Baltimorians love. The illustration above shows how the steaming process changes the color of Blue Crabs outer shell. Blue Crabs are sold by the dozen and are often served at large social gatherings. The process to retreive the meat from the crustaceans can be a bit tedious, but, in my opionion, the process is well worth it because Blue Crab meat is delicious.

Inner Harbor

The Inner Harbor is the centerpiece of Baltimore. It is home to a myriad of fantastic restraunts, museums, shops, and businesses that offer fantastic views of the Patapsco River. My favorite attraction in the Inner Harbor is the National Aquarium (located on the right in the image above). The Aquarium is homw to thousands of species of marine life and features a fantastic dolphin show in which six dolphin perform amazing tricks for patrons. If you're looking for something fun and unique to do in the city of Baltimore, look no further than the inner harbor!

Baltimore Ravens

Of course I'm a Dawg fan now, but I've been a Ravens fan my entire life. Established in 1996, the Baltimore Ravens is a realatively new NFL franchise. However, in their first 20 seasons, the Ravens have been very successful, winning dozens of playoff games and two Superbowls. Ever since the franchise's inception, the Ravens have had a reputation as team that values defensive success over everything else. The Ravens reputation for excellent defensive play is due in large part to Ed Reed and Ray Lewis, two Hall of Fame caliber defensive players who led the team from the late 1990s to 2012. Ravens football is enormously important to Baltimorians. On Sundays in the fall, you can expect nearly all TVs in town to be tuned to the Ravens game.
